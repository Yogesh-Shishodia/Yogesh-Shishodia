The Real Problem

The Problem with
Traditional BI

Traditional BI means expensive tools that need specialist configuration, dashboards nobody reads, data that's always slightly out of date, and insights buried in charts that require interpretation before anyone can act on them.

Most business decisions are still made on gut feel because the data is technically accessible but practically unusable. A chart showing revenue by region doesn't tell you why it changed or what to do about it.

Power BI, Tableau, Looker — they're powerful visualisation tools. But visualisation is not intelligence. Showing you a graph still puts all the cognitive work on you.

Our approach is different.

AI reads the data and tells you what it means, what changed, and what to do — in plain language. No charts to interpret. No dashboards to build. No analyst needed to bridge the gap between data and decision.

What We Build

What AI Business Intelligence Does

Six core capabilities that turn your raw operational data into daily, actionable intelligence — without a single analyst or dashboard.

📊

Automated Daily Reports

AI pulls data from all sources each morning, analyses it, and delivers a plain-English summary: what's up, what's down, what needs your attention today — sent to your inbox or Slack before 9am.

🚨

Anomaly Detection

AI monitors your KPIs continuously and alerts you the moment something unusual happens — a sales drop, a spike in support tickets, an unexpected churn cluster — before it snowballs into a real problem.

🔮

Predictive Forecasting

Based on historical patterns, AI forecasts: next month's revenue, 30-day churn risk, inventory needs, cash flow position. Know what's coming before it arrives, with enough time to act.

🗣️

Natural Language Querying

Ask "Why did revenue drop in August?" or "Which customers are most at risk of churning?" — and get a real answer, not a chart to interpret. Plain language in, plain language out.

🔍

Root Cause Analysis

When something goes wrong, AI traces back through the data to identify the contributing factors — not just what happened, but why it happened, and which specific variables changed first.

📋

Executive Dashboards

Auto-generated weekly and monthly board reports: narrative plus numbers, formatted and ready to present. Share with stakeholders who need the story, not the spreadsheet.
